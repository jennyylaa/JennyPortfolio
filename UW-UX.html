UW/UX Website

a site for our Waterloo founded student organization committed to fostering a design community

Project Type: UI/UX, Website Design

Role: Product Designer

Tools: Figma, Figjam, Notion

Timeline: Jan 2023 - April 2024

Background

UW/UX is a Waterloo-founded student organization committed to fostering a design community that allows designers to easily collaborate, improve their skills, and learn from a diverse set of viewpoints.

Project Overview

Our website team, composed of a Design Lead and four designers, was responsible for designing and developing UW/UX’s website from the ground up through weekly meetings and design sprints. Our team took an Agile approach, breaking the project into phases and emphasizing continuous collaboration and improvement.

Challenge:

How might we design an intuitive website that reflects our club’s identity while fostering an inclusive design community?

Objectives & Key Results

Objectives:

  1. Improve continuity and organization as we currently have information scattered across social media platforms
  2. Ensure that our audience reaches a variety of students from a diverse set of programs
  3. Organize our organization's information in a more accessible format

Key Results:

  1. Decrease the average time student’s spend looking for information by 15%
  2. Increase our overall student reach outside the engineering and GBDA faculties, resulting in an increase of diverse students by 10%
  3. Increase accessibility of our platforms with a specific metric of 75% approval ratings gathered from user testing

Wireframes

Each designer created low-fidelity wireframes on Figma, brainstorming various layout designs for each page.

Medium-fidelity Prototype

Each designer brainstormed ideas and created mid-fidelity prototypes in Figma, envisioning potential landing page designs. During meetings, we presented our concepts to the project manager, design lead, and fellow designers. We conducted design critique sessions in FigJam, analyzing what worked well and identifying areas for improvement. Through collaborative discussions, we shared our design decisions and provided constructive feedback to refine each iteration.

DESIGN CRITIQUE FINDINGS

Components that worked well:

  • hero image
  • curvy arrows to direct users on the page
  • reflecting UW/UX's playful brand

Elements to include in hi-fi prototype:

  • high-contrasting rounded buttons
  • neutral background for accent colour flexibility
  • having a balance of both images and illustrations

Design System

Our team created a visual design system in order to easily maintain consistency throughout each of our site pages. We collectively decided on the design of each of our primary, secondary, and tertiary buttons as well as their hover states. We developed two versions of our footer, incoroporating UW/UX's existing brand elements.

User Research

For our high-fidelity prototypes, we experimented with different colors, illustrative elements, images, and text, continuously refining our designs. Our team worked closely with the UX Research team to identify which website components needed testing.

We collaborated with the UX Research Team to conduct AB Testing of our events page.

AB TESTING OBJECTIVES

Upcoming Events

  • do users realize that the entire card is interactable?
  • do the cards contain enough information that they are expecting?
  • which hover state do they prefer?

Past Events

  • is the zoom on the pictures distracting?
  • is everything on the past event cards easily readable and clear?
  • does the past event modal contain all the info/images you would like to know/see from the event?

Final Interactive Prototype

After gathering valuable feedback, we were able to iterate our interactive prototypes, ensuring they were both visually engaging and user-friendly.

Web Development

After months of iterations, our team handed off our high-fidelity prototypes to the Web Development Team. As they built the website on Webflow, our Design Team conducted reviews and provided constructive feedback through FigJam.

Key Takeaways

1. Cross-functional collaboration

I collaborated with our project manager, design lead, UX research team, content team, and web designers to refine design solutions and deliver a cohesive website.

2. Feedback at every stage

Constructive feedback was regularly shared in weekly meetings, which allowed me to continuously improve and iterate my designs quickly.
